1. The Postgres "WAL" or Disk Space Wall
Since your Server, Worker, and Postgres are all on one machine, this is the #1 culprit.
What happened: Postgres uses Write-Ahead Logs (WAL). If your database reached a certain size or your server's disk space dropped below a threshold (e.g., 10%), Postgres might have switched to "Synchronous Commit" or "Read-Only" mode.
The Lock-up: When your workstation-worker tries to report a "Flow Started" status, the Prefect Server waits for Postgres to acknowledge the write. Postgres is "hanging" or "retrying" internally. The Prefect Server keeps the workstation's socket open but silent.
The Freeze: Your workstation enters the "Busy-Wait" loop we discussed, pegging the CPU and freezing the UI while waiting for the server to say "Got it."
Check: On the server, check the available disk space and look at the pg_log folder for "could not write to log file" errors.
